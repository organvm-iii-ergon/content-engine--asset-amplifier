```python
def update_brand_embedding(
    be_t: BrandEmbedding,
    positive_signal: np.ndarray,
    negative_signal: np.ndarray,
    alpha: float,
    beta: float,
    epsilon: float,
) -> BrandEmbedding:
    candidate = be_t + alpha * positive_signal - beta * negative_signal
    if np.linalg.norm(candidate - be_t) > epsilon:
        candidate = be_t + epsilon * (candidate - be_t) / np.linalg.norm(candidate - be_t)
    return candidate
```

# VI. CONVERGENCE AND STABILITY CLAIMS

You explicitly asked for proof hardening, including convergence properties of BE/NC updates. The correct claim is **bounded stability**, not absolute convergence.

## VI.1 Brand Embedding Stability

Let:

\[
BE_{t+1}=BE_t+\alpha \Delta_+ - \beta \Delta_-
\]

with projection:

\[
\|BE_{t+1}-BE_t\|\le \epsilon
\]

Then the embedding update sequence is bounded. If the update signals decay over time and the projection is fixed, the sequence is Cauchy-like in practice and will settle into a small neighborhood rather than diverging.

That is the strongest honest claim available without imposing stronger assumptions.

## VI.2 Natural Center Stability

Because NC is partly symbolic and partly vectorized, do not claim point convergence. Claim **versioned bounded drift**:

\[
D(NC_t,NC_{t+1}) \le \epsilon_{NC}
\]

with

\[
D = \lambda_1 D_T + \lambda_2 D_A + \lambda_3 D_{\Tau} + \lambda_4 D_N + \lambda_5 D_S + \lambda_6 D_{\Omega}
\]

This can be implemented as a mixed symbolic-vector distance.

## VI.3 Strategy Weight Stability

If learning weights update as:

\[
w_{k,t+1}=w_{k,t}+\eta g_{k,t}
\]

and you clip them into a simplex or bounded interval, then strategy weights are bounded. That is sufficient for production safety.

# VII. REGRET BOUNDS FOR FRAGMENT SELECTION

You asked for regret hardening. The correct operational move is to frame selection as a contextual bandit after bootstrap.

At V1, selection is deterministic top-\(N\) by score with diversity constraints. fileciteturn2file13  
At V2, upgrade to:

\[
a_t = \arg\max_f \hat{r}(f \mid context_t) + bonus_t(f)
\]

Then cumulative regret is:

\[
R_T=\sum_{t=1}^{T}\left(r(f_t^*)-r(f_t)\right)
\]

You do not yet have enough structure from the sources to claim tight formal regret bounds. The honest bridge requirement is:

```text
Implement contextual bandit interfaces now.
Log all action contexts and rewards.
Claim empirical regret reduction, not closed-form theoretical regret, until the estimator family is fixed.
```
